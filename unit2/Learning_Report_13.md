# My Personal Opinions

## Not Okay
I don't believe that AI can experience ethic s or common morality. So AI should not be used in making ethical decisions that could lead to:
- Decisions involving human life
  - Medical decisions, prescriptions
  - War time decisions/military applications 
- Necessary deception including lying to the public or informing the public
- Breaking promises or social contracts
- Inflicting personal injury


## Okay
### Advising on Controversial decisions
But I do believe that it can help advise people on various ethical decisions like those listed above, but they should only advise not take action. It may be helpful to have a LLM work through possible consequences of an action and help a user weigh them before taking action. But I would feel uncomfortable not having a human in the loop.

### Programming w/ Limitations
I believe that AI can also be used for automating trivial things or reversable decisions such as deploying websites, debugging code, reviewing code. But these applications should be limited to specific instances where the AI is not in complete control of the operation.

### Trivial decision making
You know, now that we have all these tools, we ought to do something about it and make good use of this incredible technology. Like I mentioned in the past section, I like the idea of giving an AI access to tools that have operations that can be undone in the event of a poor decision. I also think that if the stakes are low enough, then it is worth handing over a few automated decisions within specific parameters. 

For example, I think of having an AI make a purchase for me online after we have discussed a few options of products that would work best for my needs. If I had an interface where I had already made the decision to do so, it would be nice to give the AI permission on my behalf to make a purchase. I could impose a hard limit to its behavior and can return the purchase if it doesn't work out.
